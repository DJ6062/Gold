Globex sweep — how it works
STEP 1 — During overnight Globex (6 PM – 8:25 AM ET), gold builds a defined range on thin volume.

STEP 2 — At NY open (8:30 AM ET), institutional flow arrives. Price frequently spikes ABOVE the Globex high or BELOW the Globex low — hunting stop orders retail traders placed outside the range.

STEP 3 — After the sweep, price violently reverses back into the range. This reversal candle is your entry signal.

ENTRY — Wait for sweep confirmation + MSS on 5m chart. Enter on the first pullback into a 5m OB or FVG. Stop above/below sweep wick. Target: opposite Globex level.

What position squaring looks like in price
Before a major event (FOMC, NFP), traders close positions to avoid being caught offside. This creates a specific visual pattern:

VOLUME ↓ — volume drops significantly in the 1–2 hours before the event.

PRICE → — price drifts sideways or mean-reverts toward VWAP or prior close with small candles.

SPREAD ↑ — bid-ask spread widens slightly as market makers hedge exposure.

DANGER: Don't interpret the drift as a breakout. It's not. Wait for the event to pass, then trade the actual institutional reaction.

// CONDITION
1.Mark Globex high and low before 8:25 AM ET every single day
2.At 8:30 AM ET NY open, price spikes above Globex high OR below Globex low
3.Spike candle closes BACK INSIDE the Globex range = sweep confirmed
// ENTRY + MANAGEMENT
4.Entry: limit at 50% of the spike candle body, or first 5m OB inside range
5.Stop: beyond the spike wick extreme
6.Target: opposite Globex level (T1), then PDH/PDL (T2)
